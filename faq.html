75,000

The number of seats in Atlanta’s Mercedes-Benz Stadium, which opened in 2017. It pales in comparison to the size of some college arenas—the University of Michigan boasts the largest football stadium in the United States, with a capacity of 107,601 people.

13

The total number of points scored in the entire game. The lowest ever in Super Bowl History.

$3.3 Million

The estimated revenue from Atlanta Airbnb rentals during Super Bowl weekend. This cost for a room through the rental service averages $170, up 112.5 percent from the normal room price of $80.

1.25 Billion

The number of chicken wings consumed on Super Bowl Sunday. 120 million pounds of avocados, four million pizzas, and 50 million cases of beer are all scarfed and guzzled on what is the second biggest eating day of the year — Thanksgiving is the first.

$4,613

The average cost of tickets for the 2019 Super Bowl. Admission to the first event in 1967 cost a mere $12, which would be about $90 today.

$5.25 Million

The cost for a 30-second ad. As of 2017, Super Bowl ads cost advertisers about $5 million for 30-second spots. When the Bowl first began, ads were a much more conservative $40,000, but they've climbed in cost every year since then.

$6 Billion

The estimate of money worldwide in both legal and illegal bets that were placed on Super Bowl LIII according to the American Gaming Association. At many sportsbooks, up to 50% of the total handle and amount wagered on the Super Bowl was expected to be on proposition bets.

98.2 Million

The actual number of people who watched the 6:32 to 10:05 PM ET game on CBS itself. The game was down just over 5% in total audience from last year’s Super Bowl on NBC with a Nielsen rating of 41.1.
